# caribbean-fertility-analysis

&gt; A concise look at how 14 Caribbean nations have moved from baby-boom to below-replacement fertility in just seven decades.

---

## Project title  
**Fertility Rate Analysis: Caribbean Region (1950 – 2023)**

---

## Analysis summary  
The chart plots the total fertility rate for 14 Caribbean countries(Antigua and Barbuda,The Bahamas,Barbados,Cuba, Dominica, Dominican Republic, Grenada,
Guyana, Haiti,Jamaica, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Lucia, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Trinidad and Tobago 

---

## Key findings  

- **Haiti stands out** – it’s the only country still above replacement level (2.656 vs the 2.1 threshold)  
- **Haiti started highest** (6.3) and dropped least in percentage terms – went from 6.3 to 2.7  
- **Jamaica had the lowest fertility rate in 2023**, seeing it go from 3.923 in 1950 to 1.358 in 2023  
- **Most islands at the end of 2023 are clustered around 1.4-1.7 births per woman** – Jamaica, Trinidad, Barbados, Cuba all ended up in similar range

## Data & methods  

* **Source**: [Our World in Data – Total Fertility Rate](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/children-born-per-woman)) (download date: 2024-01-15)
